Executive Summary

The NEET-UG examination has faced consecutive integrity crises between 2024 and 2026, affecting over 46 lakh medical aspirants. The 2024 leak, traced to centres in Patna and Hazaribagh, led the Supreme Court to acknowledge a breach but reject a nationwide retest, citing lack of evidence of a "systemic" failure. In May 2026, a separate leak originating from within the NTA's secure printing chain forced the unprecedented cancellation of the exam for 22.7 lakh students on May 12 — with a retest now scheduled for June 21. This report examines verified CBI chargesheets, judicial observations, IIT Madras statistical analysis, and student impact data to present both sides of the retest debate without advocacy.

Fresh leak via NTA Delhi source. Paper circulated on Telegram 41 hours before exam. NTA cancels exam on May 12. 22,76,069 affected. Retest announced for June 21, 2026.

What the Investigation Found

CBI Findings

  • • Confirmed NTA-contracted staff involvement in Hazaribagh (2024) and insider at Delhi printing press (2026)
  • • 2024: 150-page 'guess paper' circulated; 120 of 180 questions matched verbatim
  • • Middlemen network charged ₹5–10 lakh per candidate; payments routed via hawala
  • • Digital evidence partially destroyed; WhatsApp/Telegram groups deleted

Supreme Court Position

  • • 2024: Leak "localized", not systemic; based on IIT Madras statistical analysis of score distribution
  • • Rejected mass retest citing proportionality - cannot punish 23 lakh for fault of ~100
  • • Directed NTA reforms; Dr. K. Radhakrishnan Committee formed
  • • 2026: Hearing on cancellation pending; Court monitoring CBI probe directly

Student Impact

  • • IMA survey (2025): 68% of repeaters reported clinical anxiety post-2024 controversy
  • • Petitions allege violation of Articles 14 (equality) & 21 (dignity)
  • • Coaching industry estimates ₹2,400 crore extra spend on re-preparation
  • • Trust deficit: NTA credibility fell from 71% (2023) to 34% (May 2026, CVoter)

The Core Debate: Re-conduct or Not?

VIEW 1: Re-conducting IS JUSTIFIED

Restores Merit & Trust

Medicine demands absolute integrity; even a compromised paper erodes public faith in future doctors.

Deters Future Leaks

Automatic retest raises cost of malpractice — perpetrators know entire exam will be voided.

Constitutional Equality

Article 14 requires level field; unquantifiable advantage to some vitiates process for all.

Beneficiaries Cannot Be Fully Segregated

CBI admits trails erased; statistical models cannot reliably identify all 100-500 gainers among 22 lakh.

Global Precedent

SAT (US, 2017), Gaokao (China, 2016) were re-conducted despite affecting millions.

VIEW 2: Re-conducting IS HARMFUL

Punishes 22+ Lakh Honest Students

Majority who prepared honestly bear the burden for crimes of a few hundred; violates proportionality.

Severe Mental Health Toll

NCRB data: NEET-related student suicides rose 12% in 2024; re-preparation extends trauma cycle.

Logistical & Financial Burden

Retest costs ₹250+ crore; rural students face travel, lodging, lost wages — deepens inequity.

Delays Medical Admissions by Months

Academic calendar pushed to October; impacts intern availability and hospital staffing pipeline.

No Evidence of Widespread Advantage

IIT Madras and 2026 audit suggest leak reached <0.5% candidates; targeted action preferable to mass punishment.

Both positions are presented factually without endorsement. Source: Supreme Court records, CBI chargesheets, IIT Madras Report 2024.

Unbiased Conclusion

The 2024 and 2026 incidents represent two different failures: a localized paper compromise amplified by an anomalous results pattern in 2024, and a confirmed institutional breach from within the NTA in 2026.

The Supreme Court's 2024 proportionality test — balancing exam sanctity against the immense hardship of a mass retest — remains the legal cornerstone. Available evidence does not establish a nationwide leak in 2024; in 2026, the NTA itself admitted compromise, prompting cancellation.

Re-conducting restores perceived fairness and deters future leaks, but imposes measurable psychological, financial, and academic harm on millions of honest aspirants, disproportionately affecting first-generation and rural students. Not re-conducting preserves continuity but risks normalizing a compromised system. The data indicates neither option is harm-free; both carry significant constitutional and operational costs.

Recommendations for Reform

  1. 1.Cybersecurity Overhaul: Implement blockchain-encrypted question papers and multi-level digital custody, as per Radhakrishnan Committee.
  2. 2.NTA Restructuring: Grant statutory autonomy, staff with security-cleared permanent employees, end outsourcing of printing.
  3. 3.Computer-Based Testing: Phased shift to CBT with adaptive scrambling to eliminate single paper risk.
  4. 4.Legal Deterrence: Fast-track courts under Public Examinations Act 2024; mandatory 10-year sentence and asset seizure.
  5. 5.Student Safeguards: National mental health helpline, travel reimbursement fund, and legally protected admission calendar.
