Section 1

Education

Education in Crisis – NEET-UG 2026 Cancelled

What happened: The National Testing Agency cancelled the NEET-UG held on 3 May after confirmed paper leak allegations. The CBI registered a case on 12 May; a re-exam is set for 21 June. The government announced NEET will move to computer-based testing from 2027.

Scale: Approximately 23 lakh candidates across 551 cities were affected. Leaked papers were allegedly sold for ₹2-25 lakh through organized networks operating on Telegram and WhatsApp.

Investigation: 9+ arrests including a Pune professor, with searches across Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Haryana. Reports indicate 135 of 180 questions matched the leaked material circulated 48 hours before the exam.

Why it matters: This is the second major NTA failure after the 2024 NEET controversy. It erodes trust in central exams, creates severe mental and financial stress for families, and delays the entire medical admission calendar. A Supreme Court plea now seeks a complete NTA overhaul.

Outlook: A CBT shift may reduce physical leaks but raises significant digital divide concerns for students from Tier-2/3 cities with poor testing infrastructure.

Banking – PSBs Hit Historic High

Numbers: PSB net profit rose 11.2% YoY to ₹1.98 trillion in FY26, marking the fourth straight profitable year. SBI led with ₹80,032 crore, followed by Bank of Baroda at ₹20,021 crore and PNB at ₹16,904 crore.

Drivers: Strong corporate loan demand, recoveries from written-off accounts via IBC, and treasury gains. Asset quality improved sharply across all 12 PSBs.

Section 3

Markets

Markets & Macro – Selloff and Rupee Pressure

Tuesday 13 May: Sensex fell 1,456 points to 74,559, Nifty dropped 436 points to 23,380 – the worst single-day fall since March. Selling was led by FPIs in financials and IT.

Currency & Oil: The rupee opened at ~95.6 and later touched a record intraday low of 96.25-96.35 during the week. Brent crude traded in a $107-111 range, up nearly 18% month-on-month.

Policy response: The government hiked gold and silver import duty to 15% (about 18% effective with cess) to curb non-essential dollar demand. The RBI intervened aggressively via spot and forward dollar sales, estimated at over $8 billion.

Analysis: The combination of FPI outflows ($3.2bn in May so far) plus an oil price shock is creating twin-deficit stress. The rupee has fallen 5.5% since the Iran-Israel war escalated in late April, threatening imported inflation.

Section 4

Energy

Energy Security – $20 Billion Offshore Push

Announcement: Petroleum Minister Hardeep Puri announced India will invest over $20 billion in offshore seismic data acquisition under the 'Samudra Manthan' mission.

Scope: The plan covers 150,000 line km across Bengal-Purnea, Mahanadi, Krishna-Godavari, Cauvery, and Andaman basins. ONGC has floated an $18-20 billion deepwater rig tender for 8-10 rigs.

Context: India imports ~85% of its crude oil. The mission targets 100 exploratory wells per year and aims to raise domestic reserves from 1.6 to 2 billion tonnes by 2035.

  • Why now: Iran war has pushed Brent above $111, exposing vulnerability.
  • Strategic petroleum reserves to be expanded from 74 days to 90-day import cover.
  • Focus on gas-rich Andaman to reduce LNG import dependence.

Section 5

Geopolitics

Geopolitics – Hormuz Toll Threat

Iran's move: On 16 May, Iranian lawmaker Ebrahim Azizi said Iran is designing a mechanism to "manage Hormuz traffic," with fees for navigation services. He stated only vessels from "cooperating" nations would benefit.

Diplomatic track: Iran FM Abbas Araghchi, visiting Delhi on 14 May, assured that Hormuz would remain open to all except nations at war with Iran, and promised "special facilitation" for Indian tankers and insurance.

Oman response: Oman, which co-manages the strait, rejected any unilateral fees, citing UNCLOS transit passage rights which guarantee free navigation.

Implication for India: Over 60% of India's crude and 45% of LNG passes through Hormuz. Even a modest $50,000-100,000 per tanker fee would add $1.5-2 billion annually to the import bill, directly justifying the urgency of Samudra Manthan.
