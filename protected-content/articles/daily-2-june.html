Nvidia unveils chip to 'reinvent' PCs in AI era

NvidiaComputex1 June

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang unveiled the RTX Spark PC chip in Taipei — a CPU+GPU in a single package built with MediaTek that will run Windows on Arm this fall, aimed at putting AI capabilities directly into laptops and desktops.

Nvidia's RTX Spark – Features:

  • CPU + GPU in single chip, built with MediaTek
  • 6,144 Blackwell GPU cores
  • NVLink technology
  • Vera CPU designed for AI agents, early access to Anthropic and SpaceX

The product is part of Nvidia's three-year "reinvent the PC" collaboration with Microsoft. The RTX Superchip combines Nvidia's central processing unit with 6,144-core Blackwell graphics processor; both share built-in memory to handle large AI models and high-end games.

Manufactured by TSMC on 3nm process. Huang called Vera "our new major growth driver," targeting the $200 billion PC market. "This (Vera CPU) is going to be our new major growth driver"

Market reaction: Intel fell 7% pre-market, Nvidia up 2%, Arm Holdings up >14% as investors bet Nvidia's entry will loosen Intel's stranglehold on PC chips.

China developing AI tool to predict who could pose political risk

ChinaSurveillanceGeedge

Chinese firm Geedge Networks is developing AI-powered technology to not just monitor dissent but predict who could become a dissident in the future — a "dystopian" capability, according to researchers.

Geedge, which sells a commercial version of the Great Firewall, is building behavioural profiles using social media, internet usage, telecommunications and location data to flag attempts to bypass censorship and highlight "political risk."

Tech risks:

  • System analyses social media to build behavioural profiles
  • US export controls on advanced AI chips have slowed progress
  • No evidence of full deployment, but experts warn it could strengthen authoritarian control

Documents (100,000 from Geedge, posted Sep 2024) show the company has exported network security software to Ethiopia, Kazakhstan, Myanmar and Pakistan. Researchers say Biden-era chip controls hampered development; Trump administration has weakened some controls, potentially giving China access to more advanced Nvidia processors.

The effort mirrors GoLaxy's work on AI-powered propaganda. Officials acknowledge Chinese firms are racing toward predictive policing, but computing power constraints remain if tools incorporate phone intercepts or video surveillance.

2 GEOPOLITICS & ENERGY

Iran to suspend indirect talks with US over Israel attacks

Iran-USOil1 June

Iran is halting indirect negotiations with the US after Israel ordered troops deeper into Lebanon to battle Hezbollah, Iranian agency Tasnim said Monday, complicating efforts to end three months of war.

Oil prices jumped more than $5 a barrel after the "ceasefire violation." Tasnim said Tehran had attacked a US base following weekend US strikes on Iranian military targets.

The US military said it struck an Iranian ground control station and two drones after "aggressive Iranian actions," including shooting down a US drone over international waters. Iran's IRGC said it targeted a US air base in response to an attack on southern Iran; Kuwait activated air defences Monday, denouncing the attacks.

President Trump, in a late-night post, said Iran "really wants to make a deal," calling critics "seemingly unpatriotic" for negative chirping. Iranian FM Abbas Araghchi called the ceasefire violation on all fronts and said the US and Israel are responsible.

Houthis threatened to "punish Israel" by closing the Bab el-Mandeb Strait. Tasnim said "there will be no talks until Iran and the resistance's views on this matter are met."

3 ECONOMY & AGRICULTURE

Govt slashes expenditure by ₹60K cr to meet deficit goal

FiscalFY26

The Centre has slashed total expenditure by around ₹60,000 crore in FY26 below the Revised Estimate to meet the 4.4% of GDP fiscal deficit target, CGA data released Monday show.

Fiscal math: Revenue expenditure cut ₹26,636 cr, capital expenditure cut ₹33,055 cr — total expenditure down ₹59,691 cr to ₹49.1 trillion. Total receipts also fell ₹20,368 cr to ₹33.6 trillion due to shortfall in personal income tax and GST.

Provisional fiscal deficit: ₹15.32 trillion (vs RE ₹15.59 trillion). ICRA's Aditi Nayar said the 4.4% target was met aided by ₹0.6 trillion lower expenditure, offsetting the revenue miss.

April FY27 data show fiscal deficit at 21.4% of full-year target vs 11.9% last year, with sharp rises in tax and non-tax revenues and spending. Risks remain from higher fertiliser and LPG subsidies, excise duty shortfalls, and West Asia uncertainty — estimated at 0.5% of GDP for FY27.

Govt scales down fertiliser requirement

AgricultureKharif 2026

The government has scaled down kharif 2026 fertiliser requirement to 38.39 million tonnes from 39.05 mt projected earlier, citing El Niño concerns.

Revised needs: Urea 19.03 mt (down 1.9% from 19.4), DAP 5.62 mt (down 4.9% from 5.91). Total down 1.7%.

India currently holds 19.98 mt in stock — about 52% of reassessed requirement — reflecting advance stocking. Around 13.24 mt has been added via imports and domestic production since the West Asia crisis, with 2.5 mt of urea produced domestically in May (+0.28 mt YoY).

IMD's second-stage forecast lowered monsoon to 90% of LPA (from 92%), due to El Niño — potentially India's driest monsoon in more than a decade. The Indian Ocean Dipole remains neutral. The country has already secured ~2.5 mt urea, 1.5 mt DAP and 1 mt NPKs through tenders arriving June-July.
