Google Cloud India growth to be driven by agentic AI, data: MD

Google Cloud28 May

Google Cloud India's next phase will be led by four "transformative" bets — enterprise AI, agentic AI, data modernisation and security — MD Sashikumar Sreedharan told Business Standard.

"The customer is modernising data, overhauling security completely, bringing in agentic AI. Before this, they were deeply exploring generative use-case models," he said.

In India, the combination of Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, agentic data cloud and defence-sector model play has helped Google gain an edge against hyperscalers entrenched in enterprise and public sector.

A semantic layer in the cloud solves the fragmentation problem: "As long as it (data) can be indexed, the AI instrument will absolutely make sense of all of them together."

Sreedharan, appointed in 2025, sees early adoption in financial services with high data volumes, and horizontal wins in call centres. Complex workflows like health insurance claims will need multiple talking agents. Manufacturing pilots — agents on factory floors — are emerging but "nobody is rushing into it. They're looking at one element at a time."

TCS partners with Mistral for enterprise AI deployment

TCSMistral AI

TCS has become the first global system integrator for Mistral Forge, the French frontier-model platform, to help enterprises build and scale custom AI models.

The partnership combines Mistral's models with TCS' domain and engineering capabilities. TCS will establish a dedicated Centre of Excellence to drive joint innovation and give clients early access to Mistral's beta models.

Initial focus sectors: banking and financial services, manufacturing, healthcare and public sector — where trusted AI adoption is becoming critical.

"THE PARTNERSHIP WITH MISTRAL REINFORCES TCS' COMMITMENT TO SCALING ENTERPRISE AI WITH TRUST, CONTROL AND MEASURABLE BUSINESS OUTCOMES AT THE CORE" — K Krithivasan, CMD

Arthur Mensch, Mistral CEO, said the collaboration will help enterprises "move from experimentation to AI deployment with systems that are open, production-ready and aligned with their strategic and operational requirements."

Indian enterprises shifting from PoC to agentic AI rollout

AWSAmazon Quick

Indian enterprises are moving from AI pilots to production deployment, said Jigar Thakkar, VP, Amazon Quick, at the AWS Summit Mumbai.

AWS is pitching Quick as a platform-agnostic agentic AI layer that works across Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Zoom and Slack — unlike rivals tied to proprietary ecosystems.

Key differentiator: Quick builds a "knowledge graph" and "context graph" around users, projects and workflows, enabling agents to understand organisational context and execute tasks across apps.

Thakkar noted Quick can run as a standalone app — "You can download it and run it at your home without even creating an AWS account." Customers like BMW, 3M and Mondelez have started using it.

AWS has committed $12.7bn in India infra by 2030, with data centres in Mumbai and Hyderabad. Global AI services revenue run rate hit $15 billion in Q1 2026.

CIL to invest ₹60,000 cr in gasification projects

Coal IndiaEnergy Security

Coal India plans ₹50,000-60,000 crore of cumulative investment in three coal gasification projects to produce chemicals and synthetic fuels, reducing import dependence amid West Asia disruptions.

The Centre on 13 May approved a ₹37,500 crore incentive scheme for gasifying 75 MT of coal/lignite, part of India's 100 MT by 2030 target.

  • Lakhanpur, Odisha: most advanced, ~₹20,000 cr, based on indigenous tech, to produce ammonium nitrate; on-ground work started; operational by 2029-30
  • Chandrapur, Maharashtra: WCL + BPCL, to produce synthetic natural gas (SNG)
  • Bardhaman, West Bengal: with GAIL, also SNG

The other two projects are at tendering stage, expected by 2030-31, each ~₹20,000 cr. CIL is evaluating European and Chinese technologies — "technology has matured a lot in China," an official said. Tenders could be finalised in June.

Plans afoot to counter El Niño threat to kharif crops: Chouhan

AgricultureMonsoon

Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan said the Centre will make district-wise contingency plans as IMD forecasts a below-normal southwest monsoon for 2026 (~92% of LPA) and WMO/NOAA flag El Niño emerging May-June.

"GOVERNMENT WILL ALSO SOON LAUNCH A NATIONWIDE 'KHET BACHAO ABHIYAN' TO PROMOTE THE BALANCED USE OF FERTILISERS"

El Niño typically brings drier, hotter conditions. The ministry is identifying alternative crops and ensuring seed availability. Chouhan said fertiliser supplies are secured despite West Asia crisis.

Despite risks, India is on track for record foodgrain output of 376.56 million tonnes in 2025-26 (+18.8 MT). ICAR DG M L Jat said rice targets for 2047 are met, urging diversification: cut rice area from 50 to 35 million hectares by 2047 and shift to oilseeds and pulses for self-reliance. He flagged 100+ districts over-using fertilisers.

Iran and US trade air strikes as hopes for Hormuz deal falter

Iran-USHormuz

The US and Iran reached an outline 60-day MoU to extend their ceasefire, Axios reported, but the deal awaits President Trump's signoff after a fresh exchange of strikes.

Iran targeted a US base in Kuwait after American strikes near Bandar Abbas. US forces shot down five Iranian drones and struck a control station. Kuwait called it a "serious escalation."

Oil prices rebounded >2% after falling 5%, as confidence in a deal faded. Trump said: "THEY (IRANIAN LEADERS) THOUGHT THEY WERE GOING TO OUTWAIT ME... I DON'T CARE ABOUT THE MIDTERMS"

Iran's IRGC said it stopped two vessels in 24 hours; pre-war traffic was >100 ships daily. Trump dismissed reports of joint Iran-Oman control of Hormuz: "It's international waters, and everybody else or we'll have to blow them up."

Tehran insists on US releasing frozen funds and withdrawing troops from the immediate vicinity — dismissed by the White House as "complete fabrication."

The promise of Quad's geoeconomic turn

QuadGeoeconomics

Quad foreign ministers met in Delhi this week to revitalise the grouping around economic security after doubts about US focus on the Western Hemisphere and the Iran war.

Three big moves:

  • Critical minerals: Framework to build diversified supply chains, building on the Mineral Security Partnership. Aim to mobilise up to $20 billion in public-private capital and "avoid reliance on any one country" (read China)
  • Energy security: Joint statement links Indo-Pacific energy shocks from Hormuz closure to maritime route protection. Quad to focus on tech, policy, market analysis and emergency response, and convene a fuel security forum
  • Ports & connectivity: Agreement to develop a port in Fiji, expand Quad infrastructure fellowships to 2,200, and link Pacific Island countries via undersea cables

Author Sankalp Gurjar warns the real test is delivery — many past Quad initiatives fizzled — and keeping the US engaged as priorities shift.
