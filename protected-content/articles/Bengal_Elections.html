West Bengal 2026 Election: Deep Dive into Challenges, Illegal Voters & SIR Purge

How an unprecedented Special Intensive Revision, dueling fraud allegations, and administrative upheaval preceded the BJP's historic victory ending 15 years of TMC rule.

Published: June 2026 • Analysis based on ECI data & press reporting

Executive Summary

  • The Election Commission ordered 483 official transfers and central agencies reported ₹510 crore in seizures — the largest pre-poll intervention in Bengal’s history.
  • A Special Intensive Revision (SIR) altered roughly 12% of the electorate, becoming the central flashpoint of the election.
  • BJP alleges SIR removed 79 lakh "fake" voters linked to infiltration, while TMC and CPI(M) allege SIR wrongfully deleted 27.16 lakh legitimate voters, mostly Muslims.
  • Poll violence deaths fell to 4 from 17 in 2021, but West Bengal still accounts for 354 poll-related incidents since 2016 — the highest in India.
  • Booth Level Officers reported widespread harassment, complicating door-to-door verification during SIR.

The Illegal Voter Problem

The SIR triggered competing narratives of electoral fraud. Both sides presented mutually exclusive claims of manipulation. The following are presented as partisan allegations, not established facts, and remain disputed in courts and political debate.

Subsection A — BJP Allegations

Claims of Fake Voters & Infiltration

  • Leader of Opposition Suvendu Adhikari claims SIR removed 79 lakh fake names from electoral rolls.
  • BJP alleges EPIC cards were found sealed in a Bidhannagar TMC office, along with land deeds stored in a briefcase.
  • Party workers allege 100+ Aadhaar/PAN cards were discarded in a Birbhum field near the residence of a former BLO.
  • Home Minister Amit Shah alleges CM Mamata Banerjee abetted infiltration for votes, citing BSF arrest of 7 Bangladeshi nationals on May 12, 2026 in Cooch Behar.
Subsection B — TMC / CPI(M) Counter-Allegations

Claims of Disenfranchisement & Outsiders

  • TMC alleges BJP used Form 6 to add outsiders from Bihar, UP, Rajasthan, and Haryana — claiming 30,000 applications were filed in 6 hours.
  • Citing Frontline, TMC claims SIR disenfranchised 27.16 lakh voters, mostly Muslims.
  • CPI(M) alleges 12% of voters were deleted algorithmically, reversing the burden of proof onto citizens.
  • In Murshidabad (Shamsherganj, Lalgola) — district with highest deletions — TMC claims 6 men wrongly labeled as Bangladeshis were forced to vote to prove citizenship.
Note: Election Commission has not published final verified data on deletions/additions by religion or origin. All figures above are political claims.

Was the BJP Win Shocking?

Analysts remain divided. The result broke a 47-year incumbency pattern in Bengal, but followed a decade-long BJP vote share climb.

1
Unbroken Incumbency Record: TMC won 213 seats in 2021 with 48% vote share. No ruling party had lost Bengal since the Left's defeat in 2011, and before that, since 1977.
2
No Local Face & Recent Losses: BJP contested without a declared Bengali CM candidate and had lost 5 of 7 municipal corporation polls in 2024-25, including Asansol and Siliguri.
3
Polls Predicted a Tight Race: CVoter, ABP-CVoter and India Today-MyAxis surveys as late as March 2026 forecast a hung assembly or a narrow TMC edge (145-160 seats).
1
15-Year Anti-Incumbency: The Sandeshkhali protests, school recruitment scam charges, and civic issues eroded TMC's support among women and urban middle-class voters who had been its core base.
2
SIR Reshaped the Electorate: With 12% of rolls revised and highest deletions in TMC strongholds like Murshidabad, the structural composition of the voter base changed before campaigning began.
3
Steady Vote Share Growth: BJP's trajectory was consistent — from 10.2% (2016 Assembly) to 38.1% (2021) to 40.7% in the 2024 Lok Sabha in Bengal. 2026 completed the arc, not broke it.

Impact on Democracy

Regardless of partisan claims, the 2026 election leaves four structural challenges for West Bengal's electoral future.

1. Trust in Rolls Large-scale revisions months before polling risk voter confidence. When 1 in 8 names changes, legitimacy questions outlast results.
2. Citizenship as Campaign Issue Voting rights are increasingly tied to proof of citizenship and migration status, shifting the debate from development to identity documentation.
3. Institutional Polarization Both BJP and TMC publicly accused the ECI, BSF, state police, and BLOs of partisanship — deepening public skepticism of neutral arbiters.
4. Federal Precedent Record central force deployment, financial seizures, and bureaucratic transfers set a new template for Centre-State contestation in future state elections.

Conclusion

The West Bengal 2026 election was not decided on polling day alone. It was shaped by the Special Intensive Revision — an administrative exercise that both cleaned and contested the voter list, depending on whom you ask.

BJP’s victory is legally conclusive but politically contested. TMC’s allegations of mass disenfranchisement and BJP’s allegations of decades-long infiltration cannot both be fully true, yet neither has been independently adjudicated with full data transparency.

What is needed now: a district-wise, booth-level audit of SIR additions and deletions by the ECI, release of anonymized deletion reasons, and a time-bound judicial review of Form 6 bulk applications and alleged fake EPIC seizures. Without this, the "illegal voter" question will remain a weapon for 2029, not a resolved fact for 2026.
