1 TECHNOLOGY & CYBER

Anthropic expands Project Glasswing to India, 14 others

AnthropicCybersecurity3 June

IPO-bound Anthropic has expanded its cyberdefence plan Project Glasswing to 150 new organisations across 15 countries, including India, the company said.

The new cohort covers industries not represented in the initial group — power, water, healthcare, communications, and hardware. Many partners are vendors and nonprofits that maintain critical codebases relied upon by governments worldwide.

The move comes weeks after Nasscom and India's Ministry of Electronics and IT sought access to Claude Mythos. In April, Anthropic partnered with AWS, Apple, Microsoft, Google, Nvidia, Palo Alto Networks and others to deploy Mythos for defensive purposes.

Anthropic says Mythos Preview has already found thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities across every major OS and browser. The company warns: "Cheap, fast AI models with powerful cyber capabilities are around the corner. We want Project Glasswing to spur institutions toward operating norms that reflect this reality."

Within 6-12 months, Anthropic expects other AI firms to release Mythos-level models without safeguards. It is working to release such capabilities for general access but stresses robust safeguards are needed, as frontier models will increasingly empower attackers and defenders alike.

2 GEOPOLITICS

Iran strikes Kuwait Int'l Airport

IranGulf3 June

Gulf hostilities flared as Iranian drone and missile attacks damaged Kuwait International Airport, killing one and injuring more than 60, while the US carried out strikes near the Strait of Hormuz.

Flights were suspended, then resumed from Terminal 4 after damage assessment. Oil prices rose more than 2% as the strait remains largely closed three months after US-Israel strikes on Iran.

Bahrain said it intercepted three missiles and several drones targeting the US Fifth Fleet headquarters. The US military said two Iranian missiles aimed at Kuwait fell short, and it downed drones targeting civilian ships and forces in Kuwait.

In a podcast, President Trump said Iran had agreed not to have a nuclear weapon: "They've already agreed they're not going to have a nuclear weapon" — adding Khamenei was involved and "not doing too well" but giving approval. Trump said he'd like to meet Khamenei.

Iran's Revolutionary Guards said they targeted a vessel in response to a US attack on an Iranian tanker. UAE adviser Anwar Gargash called for a firm, unified Gulf response, warning aggression "does not target one country alone, but all of us."

Ukraine hits Russian oil terminal

UkraineRussia

Ukrainian long-range drones struck an oil terminal in St. Petersburg — more than 1,000 km from Ukraine — setting it ablaze, President Zelenskyy said Wednesday, as Russia hosts its annual economic forum.

Clouds of black smoke rose over the city, Putin's birthplace and Russia's second-largest. The airport briefly suspended flights overnight and authorities cut mobile internet services.

Russian authorities confirmed only that a drone targeted city infrastructure. The strike is an embarrassment for Putin, weeks after he scaled back Moscow's Victory Day parade over drone fears. Putin is set to speak Friday at the St. Petersburg forum, which the Kremlin views as prestigious.

3 DOMESTIC

11 foreigners among 21 killed in Delhi hotel fire

DelhiFire Safety

A devastating blaze tore through the Flourish Stay B&B in Malviya Nagar's Hauz Rani, killing at least 21 people including 11 foreigners, after the five-storey building — operating without a fire NOC — became a "virtual death trap."

The hotel had only one entry-exit, permanently sealed windows and a sensor-operated main door. Fire started around 8:30 am and spread rapidly. At least 58 were rescued; 7 remain critical.

Lieutenant Governor ordered a month-long drive from June 4 to check fire safety at all hotels, nursing homes, coaching institutes and restaurants. "All hotels, lodges, inns exceeding permitted limit will be closed," officials said.

Investigations found the building was licensed in 2024 under Delhi's Bed & Breakfast scheme for just six rooms (Silver category, valid till 2027), but was operating nearly four times that capacity. The structure, originally ground+1, had added floors without approved plans and exceeded 15 metres — making fire clearance mandatory.

Tourism Minister Kapil Mishra said Delhi will withdraw the B&B policy entirely. Police filed an FIR for culpable homicide; the owner and partners are absconding, with lookout alerts issued.
