Basic Economics Terms Every IMBA Student Must Know

Micro, Macro, and Indian Economy Essentials - IMBA Ready

These 42 terms cover 90% of your Class 12 Economics theory. Learn them with examples, not just definitions — boards ask for application, and these concepts stay useful for life, from understanding budget news to making personal money decisions.

1 Microeconomics Basics

Demand
Quantity consumers are willing to buy at a given price.
Eg: At ₹20 you buy 5 colas, at ₹30 only 2.
Supply
Quantity producers are willing to sell at a given price.
Eg: Farmers supply more wheat when market price rises.
Elasticity
How much demand/supply responds to price change.
Eg: Petrol is inelastic, pizza is elastic.
Opportunity Cost
Value of the next-best alternative forgone.
Eg: Choosing CA coaching means losing time for a part-time job.
PPC
Production Possibility Curve shows max combos of two goods an economy can produce.
Eg: Guns vs Butter trade-off.
Utility
Satisfaction derived from consuming a good.
Eg: First ice cream gives high utility.
Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility
Each extra unit gives less additional satisfaction.
Eg: 3rd slice of pizza less enjoyable than 1st.
Market Equilibrium
Point where demand equals supply.
Eg: At ₹500, buyers want 1000 phones = sellers sell 1000.
Price Ceiling / Floor
Ceiling = max legal price (below equilibrium). Floor = min price (above).
Eg: Rent control = ceiling; MSP for crops = floor.
Consumer Surplus
Difference between what you're willing to pay and what you actually pay.
Eg: Willing ₹120, pay ₹80, surplus = ₹40.

2 Macroeconomics Basics

GDP
Total value of final goods/services produced within country in a year.
GDP = C + I + G + (X - M)
Eg: India's GDP ~ $3.7 trillion.
GNP
GDP + Net Factor Income from Abroad.
GNP = GDP + NFIA
Eg: Includes income of Indians working in Dubai.
NNP
GNP minus Depreciation. Also called National Income at market price.
NNP = GNP - Depreciation
Eg: After deducting wear-tear of machines.
Inflation
Sustained rise in general price level.
Eg: CPI inflation 6% means basket costlier by 6%.
Deflation
Sustained fall in general price level.
Eg: Common during deep recession.
Fiscal Deficit
Govt. expenditure exceeds receipts (excluding borrowings).
FD = Total Exp - (Revenue Receipts + Non-debt Capital)
Monetary Policy
RBI's actions to control money supply and interest rates.
Eg: Hike rates to control inflation.
Repo Rate
Rate at which RBI lends short-term money to banks.
Eg: Repo 6.5% = banks borrow at 6.5%.
CRR
Cash Reserve Ratio: % of deposits banks keep with RBI as cash.
Eg: CRR 4.5% means ₹4.5 of ₹100 locked.
SLR
Statutory Liquidity Ratio: % kept in liquid assets like gold, govt bonds.
Eg: SLR 18% ensures bank safety.

3 Money & Banking

Commercial Bank
Profit-making bank that accepts deposits and gives loans.
Eg: SBI, HDFC, ICICI.
Central Bank (RBI)
Apex authority; issues currency, controls credit, banker to govt.
Eg: RBI decides repo rate.
Money Supply M1-M4
Measures of money. M1=C+DD (narrow), M3=M1+Time Deposits (broad money, most used).
Credit Creation
Banks create deposits many times initial deposit via loans.
Multiplier = 1/CRR
Bank Rate
Long-term rate RBI charges banks for loans (vs repo = short-term).
Open Market Operations
RBI buying/selling govt securities to control liquidity.
Eg: Sells bonds to reduce money supply.
Demonetization
Withdrawing legal tender status of currency notes.
Eg: India, Nov 2016 – ₹500/₹1000 banned.
Digital Payment
Cashless transfer via UPI, NEFT, RTGS, cards.
Eg: PhonePe, GPay, IMPS.

4 Indian Economy & Policy

Five Year Plans
Centralised planning by Planning Commission (1951-2017).
Eg: 1st Plan focused on agriculture.
NITI Aayog
Replaced Planning Commission in 2015; policy think-tank.
Eg: Prepares SDG India Index.
GST
Goods and Services Tax – one nation one indirect tax since 2017.
Eg: Slabs 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%.
Disinvestment
Govt selling its stake in public sector enterprises.
Eg: Sale of Air India.
Privatization
Transfer of ownership/management from public to private sector.
Liberalization
Reducing govt controls; part of 1991 LPG reforms.
Eg: Removal of industrial licensing.
FDI
Foreign Direct Investment – foreign firm invests >10% in Indian company.
Eg: Tesla plant investment.
Budget
Annual Financial Statement of govt revenue and expenditure.
Eg: Presented on Feb 1.

5 International Economics

Balance of Payments
Systematic record of all economic transactions with rest of world.
Eg: Always balanced in accounting terms.
Current Account
Records export/import of goods, services, transfers.
Eg: Deficit if imports > exports.
Capital Account
Records capital flows – FDI, FII, loans, investments.
Exchange Rate
Price of one currency in terms of another.
Eg: $1 = ₹83.
Devaluation
Official reduction in value of domestic currency by govt.
Eg: India devalued rupee in 1991.
WTO
World Trade Organization – global body for trade rules (1995).
Eg: Dispute settlement body.
