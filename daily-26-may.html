1 ECONOMY & POLICY

Sitharaman flags 3 Fs: Fuel, fertiliser, foreign exchange

FMWest Asia25 May

India's economy remains resilient but the West Asia crisis is pressuring foreign exchange reserves through high crude oil, fertiliser and gold prices, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman said at a Sidbi event in Mumbai.

"West Asia crisis can mean higher fuel costs, delayed cargo, costlier shipping, shortages of inputs, pressure on working capital, and uncertainty in export orders... We must also recognise that India's economic situation remains resilient"

The 3 Fs:

  • Fuel: Crude volatile for ~90 days; petrol up ₹2.61/litre and diesel ₹2.71/litre on 26 May — fourth hike in 10 days
  • Fertiliser: "Unimaginable increase" in international prices straining subsidy bill
  • Foreign exchange: All three items plus gold must be paid in FX — "there is no rupee trading there"

To shield consumers, Centre cut excise duty by ₹10/litre — estimated revenue hit over ₹1 trillion in 2026-27. Sitharaman called the response "very calibrated," focused on MSMEs, exporters and supply chains.

She backed the RBI's ₹2.87 trillion dividend: "I fully trust the central bank's assessment... they have made additional provisions." She also cited the >₹1 trillion Economic Stabilisation Fund created as a buffer before the crisis peaked.

On positives, she pointed to GST collections, tractor and two-wheeler sales, falling bad loans, and CII data showing private capex up 67% YoY in September 2025.

Hurdles in the Viksit Bharat race

Book ReviewJobs

A review of India Out of Work argues India needs sustained 8% growth to reach developed status by 2047, but faces a triple crisis in employment, education and economy.

  • Non-farm job creation fell from 7.7m to 4.3m annually; need is 10-12m
  • Unemployment 2014-24: vocationally trained 14%→19%, technical graduates 18%→26%, postgraduates 20%→30%
  • NEET (Not Employed, Educated or Trained) rose from 51m to 121m
  • Women's LFPR as low as Saudi Arabia and Yemen; unpaid family work up to 36.4%
  • Manufacturing share fell from 17% to 13-14% by 2024; PLI benefits concentrated (75% to Apple)

The authors call for a "dual synergies" model linking health, nutrition, water, sanitation and education to rebuild demand and human capital, rather than relying solely on supply-side schemes.

2 ENERGY & GEOPOLITICS

Hormuz fears open new energy lane between India, Canada

India-CanadaEnergy

In an interview, Canada's High Commissioner Chris Cooter said the West Asia conflict has revived fears around Hormuz and accelerated Canada-India energy cooperation following PM Carney's March 2026 visit.

Key takeaways:

  • $2.6bn uranium deal and CEPA Terms of Reference signed; CEPA Round 2 done in Delhi, Round 3 in Ottawa in July
  • Canada building west coast infrastructure to supply Asia: pipeline to west coast can deliver ~1 million barrels/day once expanded; Canada produces ~6m bpd
  • LNG from Prince Rupert targeting 50 mt/year by 2030, up to 100 mt — India currently imports ~50 mt annually
  • Cameco-DAE CAD $2.6bn deal covers 22 million pounds uranium 2027-2035 to support India's plan to grow nuclear from 8 GW to 100 GW
  • Canada to offer small modular reactors and advanced nuclear tech; "the door is open" for joint development

Cooter said the relationship is "no longer dependent solely on the Prime Minister or a joint statement" and is being insulated from Gulf risks by providing reliable supply.

Quad meet today: West Asia crisis, energy security in focus

Quad25 May

External Affairs Minister S Jaishankar hosts Quad foreign ministers — US Marco Rubio, Australia Penny Wong, Japan Toshimitsu Motegi — in New Delhi on Tuesday.

Agenda: cooperation on critical minerals, conflicts in Ukraine and West Asia, and energy security following Hormuz disruptions. The meeting aims to make Quad a semi-annual forum with "specific deliverables" for the Quad Summit India hopes to host later this year.

Rubio said the Quad's vision for a free and open Indo-Pacific would be central, calling it a forum "which are not just strategically aligned on a bunch of key issues but are having the ability to influence global events."

Conclusion reached on many issues but US deal not imminent, says Tehran

Iran-USHormuz

Iran's top negotiator was in Doha on 25 May for talks with Qatar's PM. Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei said a conclusion had been reached on many topics but "that does not mean we're close to signing an agreement."

The potential 14-point MOU focuses on ending the war and the US naval blockade of Hormuz, in exchange for Iran ensuring safe transit. Nuclear issues would be negotiated over 60 days after a framework.

Baghaei said Iran will not charge tolls but may charge for navigation services under a protocol with Oman. Iran's state TV said 32 vessels and five oil tankers passed through Hormuz in the past 24 hours with IRGC authorisation.

Trump, on Truth Social, said talks were "going nicely" and urged Saudi Arabia and Qatar to join Abraham Accords, while maintaining the blockade "will remain in full force and effect until an agreement is reached, certified and signed."

3 TECHNOLOGY & SOCIETY

Pope Leo warns of risks from AI in 42,300-word encyclical

VaticanAI Ethics

Pope Leo XIV released his first encyclical "Magnifica Humanitas" on Monday, a 42,300-word letter calling for safeguards against AI's most disruptive effects.

Pope's prescription:

  • Government regulation of private companies driving AI
  • Protection and retraining of workers whose jobs are threatened
  • Educating students to think critically about technology
  • Protecting children from violent, hypersexualised or false information
  • Ensuring humans, not AI, remain responsible for weapons decisions

Leo warned: "The pursuit of greater profits cannot justify choices that systematically sacrifice jobs" and called for "the most rigorous ethical constraints" on AI weapons. He presented the encyclical alongside Anthropic co-founder Christopher Olah as a symbol of dialogue between spiritual and technological worlds.

AI deals rising, but IT sector earnings lag

IT StocksFY27

Indian IT stocks have plunged up to 33% YTD in 2026 (to 25 May) versus Nifty 50's 8% fall, as AI-led outsourcing disrupts the traditional model.

Nifty IT scoreboard YTD: LTI Mindtree -34.2%, HCLTech -28.2%, Infosys -27.7%, TCS -27.0%, Wipro -21.4%, Persistent -19.7%. Rupee depreciation (6% vs USD in CY26) has provided some cushion.

Analysts say FY27 could be tough: deal pipelines are strong — TCS reported $12.3bn in Q4 FY26 deal wins, Infosys $5.1bn in FY26 — but conversion to revenue is slow due to pilot-led deployments and elongated decision cycles.

HCLTech reported 4.8% CC revenue growth in FY26; Coforge grew 36%. Beyond disruption, analysts see opportunities in AI data architecture and workflow agents, noting Indian IT has historically adapted well to cloud and SaaS transitions.
