Coal Energy CIL

CIL allays coal crisis fears, says 168 mt buffer available to meet rising demand

Saket Kumar

State-run Coal India Ltd said adequate coal stocks are available to meet rising summer power demand. Total coal buffer of 168 million tonnes is currently available.

Coal stocks at domestic coal-based thermal power plants stood at 47.6 mt as of May 23, while inventory at CIL's mine heads was 113.5 mt as of May 24, up 10 per cent year-on-year. Current stock level sufficient to meet around 19 days of consumption.

3 mt of coal lying at transit points, nearly 4 mt currently in transit through railway rakes. 50 mt of in-situ mine coal readily available.

Policy Rupee Capital Flows

India's policy mix needs a rethink

Ananth Narayan

Recent rupee movements warrant attention but not overreaction. Current account deficit FY27 to settle at 2.5% GDP, manageable. RBI sold $82 billion foreign exchange FY25-FY26.

India needs sustained net foreign capital inflows for growth. Policy mix has images between interest rates, taxation and external balance. Narrow interest rate differentials made it harder to attract foreign debt flows.

Lower interest rates worsened external balance. Moving towards residence-based regime for capital gains would align with global practices. Monetary policy must holistically engage impossible trinity across interest rates, capital flows, exchange rates.
